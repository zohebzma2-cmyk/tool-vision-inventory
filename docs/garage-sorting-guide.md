# How to sort a garage with Tool Vision

A field-tested process for going from a chaotic garage to a fully labeled, searchable one — in
sessions, without burning out. Works the same on desktop (tool-vision.pages.dev) and the iPhone app.

## The one rule: one zone at a time

Do **not** try to do the whole garage in a day. Pick a single wall or corner — "the pegboard wall,"
"the bin rack by the door" — finish it end to end, then stop. A finished zone is a win; a
half-sorted garage is demoralizing. Five 30-minute sessions beats one exhausting marathon.

## Before you start (once)
1. **Sign in / create your account** (top of the app). Your data syncs across your phone and
   desktop automatically.
2. **Good light.** A cheap LED light or working in daylight makes the AI far more accurate.
3. **Optional: connect a Brother label printer** (Settings → printer) if you want stick-on QR labels.
   No printer? The app still shows on-screen labels and QR codes you can photograph.

## The loop — repeat per zone

### 1. Add the space
**Spaces tab → Add space.** Name it ("Garage"), pick the type. Do this once per building
(garage, shed, basement). On iPhone you can even **3D-scan the room** for real dimensions.

### 2. Map each storage unit in the zone
For every pegboard, shelf unit, bin rack, drawer, or cabinet in the zone:
- **Spaces → Map with camera → snap it.** The AI turns it into a labeled grid of slots.
- **Not a neat grid?** After snapping, tap **Detect items** — the AI boxes each tool into its own
  spot, so irregular walls and shadow boards work too.
- Tweak the grid/spots if needed, then create it. Every slot gets its own QR code.

### 3. Fill it — two paths

**Bins of loose stuff (screws, fittings, random parts):**
- Tap the bin → **Sort bin** → **snap the inside.**
- The AI lists everything it sees **and** estimates the tote size — **confirm the size in gallons**
  (one tap).
- Review the list (fix or uncheck anything), tweak the "what's in here" summary, and **Sort & store.**
- **Print the bin label** — a plain-English summary of the contents ("Assorted plumbing fittings").

**Tools on a pegboard / shelf:**
- **Add tool → snap the tool.** The AI fills in the name, brand, and model — you just review and save.
- Assign it to a slot on the unit you mapped.

### 4. Label the real thing
Print the QR labels (Brother) or grab the on-screen ones, and stick them on the actual slots and
bins. This is what makes the garage *searchable* later.

### 5. Next unit, next zone
Move to the next storage unit in the zone, then start a fresh session on the next zone.

## After it's sorted — the payoff
- **Find anything:** search a tool by name → the app tells you the exact bin/slot.
- **Scan to jump:** scan a slot or bin's QR → see what's there (or fill it).
- **Put things back right:** scan a part's label → it shows which bin it lives in.
- **See the big picture:** the Spaces tab shows a top-down property plan; draw or AI-generate a
  blueprint of each space.

## Pro tips
- **Lay items visibly** before snapping a bin — spread them out, don't pile.
- **One bin at a time.** Dump, sort, label, done. Then the next.
- **Watch the ⚠ flags.** When sorting a bin, the app flags an item that doesn't match the rest
  ("a plumbing fitting in the electrical bin") — catch misfiles as you go.
- **Trust but verify the AI.** It's fast and usually right; the review step is there so you fix the
  occasional miss before saving.
- **Size is a rough guess.** The tote-gallon estimate is a starting point — that's why you confirm it.

## A realistic first week
- **Session 1:** Add the garage. Map + fill the one pegboard/shelf you use most.
- **Sessions 2–4:** One bin rack or drawer bank per session — sort + label each bin.
- **Session 5:** The odds-and-ends corner. Label everything. Done.

From then on, every new tool takes ~15 seconds to add, and you never lose one again.
